Why We're Here Today

  • Before the formal hub-and-spoke model, marketing operations sat within marketing. This was helpful at the time, but it often led to unreliable data, more back-and-forth, and frustration. This was built on effort, not a scalable foundation.
  • When the team was smaller, informal requests were manageable. The volume and complexity have changed.
  • Today is about catching up. Putting a clear structure in place so both teams know what to expect.

What this session is

  • A direction-setting conversation. Not a complaint session
  • A chance to build shared understanding of what each team does best
  • The beginning of a better working model. Built together

What this session is not

  • A call-out of individuals or past decisions
  • Ops telling marketing what to do
  • A fixed framework that never changes. This evolves as we learn

Getting Out of the Weeds Makes Our Lives Easier

"If we continue to sit and think in siloed ways, we're never solving for the long term."
  • We want the people who are responsible for their channels to get things done with confidence, quickly and correctly. Without bottlenecks
  • We know that part of the problem is that we're picking up tasks that channel owners can do themselves. And that fills our list, leaving us no time to give you the visibility into everything you're asking for
  • This isn't about ops doing less. It's about both teams operating at the right level

This is a structural issue. Not a people one.

Here's what we're proposing

🔄
Ops transitions out
Every repeatable execution task gets documented and handed over properly. Nothing drops without a walkthrough.
"Once it's handed over properly, analyst time is protected."
🧠
Marketing takes ownership
Each channel owner executes their own tasks — with documentation, templates, and walkthroughs provided by ops.
"The people responsible for their channels, getting things done with confidence."
🧪
Analyst time goes deep
Freed from execution, analyst time goes into infrastructure marketing can't build itself — attribution, pipeline reporting, HubSpot governance.
"The things that give marketing the full picture."
This isn't cutting support. It's directing it where it has the most leverage.

Not Sure Which Tier?

Run through these three questions first

1
How complex is this task?   Simple  ·  Medium  ·  Hard
Hard = cross-system, technical expertise needed → Tier 1. Simple = one tool, no config → likely Tier 3.
↓
2
Does this touch a system, workflow, or shared configuration?
If it involves HubSpot workflows, automations, integrations, or anything shared. Come through ops, even if it looks simple. "YouTube told me how" is not enough if it touches a live system.
↓
3
Would it take me less than 1 hour and live entirely outside shared systems?
If yes. It's self-serve. If it crosses into a system or workflow at any point, use the request form regardless of how quick it seems.
Still unsure? Submit via the request form anyway. We'll triage it. You don't need to know the tier upfront.
